TALKS

On Explainable AI and its “Unconventional” Use Fabrizio Silvestri

In this talk, we will explore some of the most recent advancements in Explainable AI (XAI) that we have developed over the past five years. We will demonstrate how XAI methods can be applied across different domains, including tabular data, graphs, and general black-box models. Specifically, we will illustrate how these techniques can be leveraged to analyze and interpret complex data structures, offering insights into decision-making processes that were previously opaque. One of the key highlights will be our novel approach to applying XAI in the context of social network analysis. We will show how these methods can be utilized to challenge community detection algorithms in social networks, enabling the modification of community structures in a way that remains undetectable to the administrators or owners of the platform. This has significant implications for the robustness and integrity of community-based mechanisms, with potential applications in privacy preservation, adversarial attacks, and the broader understanding of social dynamics within networks.
